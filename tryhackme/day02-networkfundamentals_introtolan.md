## Overview

---

## Topics Covered

### IP Addressing & Device Identification

Learned how devices are identified and communicate using:

* IP Addresses
* MAC Addresses

Understood the difference between logical and physical addressing and how both are used during communication between devices on a network.

### ARP & DHCP

Studied important networking protocols such as:

* **ARP (Address Resolution Protocol)** → used to map IP addresses to MAC addresses within a local network.
* **DHCP (Dynamic Host Configuration Protocol)** → used to automatically assign IP addresses and network configurations to devices.

These protocols helped me understand how devices connect and communicate efficiently without manual configuration.

### ICMP & Ping

Learned about:

* **ICMP (Internet Control Message Protocol)**
* How the **Ping** command works for testing connectivity between devices and checking whether a system is reachable over a network.

### Subnetting Basics

Explored the fundamentals of subnetting and learned how larger networks are divided into smaller subnetworks for:

* Better organization
* Improved security
* Efficient traffic management

Also learned about subnet masks, host ranges, and network separation.

### Networking Devices

Studied the functions of networking devices such as:

* Routers
* Switches
* Access Points

Learned how these devices manage communication and direct network traffic between systems and networks.

### Network Topologies

Explored different types of network topologies and understood how devices are arranged within a network infrastructure. Learned the purpose and structure of commonly used topologies in real-world environments.

### LAN & Internet Communication

Developed a better understanding of:

* Local Area Networks (LANs)
* How devices communicate internally
* How systems connect to external networks and the internet

Also learned how data travels between devices across networks.
